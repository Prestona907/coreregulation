The problem

Why revenue leakage goes unnoticed in fintech companies

Most billing issues are invisible until someone looks for them. Here is why they persist.

01

Undetected revenue leakage

Failed charges, pricing drift, and subscription gaps quietly erode revenue across billing systems. These issues rarely surface in day-to-day dashboards and often go unnoticed for months.

02

No visibility across processors

Most companies run Stripe alongside Adyen, PayPal, or Square. Each processor has its own dashboard, its own fee structure, and its own anomaly patterns — none of which talk to each other.

03

No audit-ready control trail

As companies scale, auditors ask deeper questions about financial controls. Informal spreadsheets don't provide the documentation required to demonstrate proper oversight during diligence.

04

Limited leadership visibility

Revenue leakage rarely appears in executive dashboards as issues sit across multiple payment systems. Leadership often assumes operations are normal while errors compound silently.
