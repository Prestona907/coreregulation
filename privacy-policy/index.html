Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 28, 2026

01

Overview

Core Regulation ("we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy and security of the information entrusted to us by our clients. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect information when you use our revenue integrity and leakage detection services.

By engaging our services or accessing our platform, you agree to the practices described in this policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of our services.

02

Information We Collect

To perform revenue leakage detection and recovery, we collect the following categories of information:

  • Payment processor data — transaction records, fee details, settlement reports, dispute data, and balance information accessed via API connections to Stripe, Adyen, Square, and PayPal.
  • Business information — company name, billing contact, and account details provided during onboarding.
  • Contact information — name, email address, and phone number of authorized representatives.
  • Usage data — how you interact with our dashboard and reports, including access logs and feature usage.
  • Communication records — emails, notes, and records related to recovery actions taken on your behalf.

03

How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect exclusively to deliver our services. Specifically:

  • Detecting revenue leakage and anomalies across your connected payment processors
  • Performing recovery actions on your behalf under your written authorization
  • Generating audit reports and findings summaries
  • Communicating with payment processors, banks, or card networks as your authorized agent
  • Maintaining an audit trail of all detection and recovery activity
  • Improving the accuracy and coverage of our detection engine
  • Sending service-related communications such as reports, alerts, and updates

We do not use your data for advertising, marketing to third parties, or any purpose unrelated to the services you have engaged us to provide.

04

API Access & Data Handling

API connections to your payment processors are established using the minimum level of access required to perform our services. For detection purposes, connections are read-only. Recovery actions — such as submitting support tickets or filing dispute evidence — require explicit written authorization from you and are performed by our team on your behalf.

We will never initiate payments, move funds, issue refunds, or make unauthorized modifications to your processor accounts. Recovered funds are always returned directly to your processor account or bank account and never pass through our systems.

API credentials provided to us are encrypted at rest using industry-standard encryption and are never stored in plain text, logged, or shared with any third party.

05

Data Sharing & Third Parties

We do not sell, rent, or share your data with third parties for their own purposes. We may share information only in the following limited circumstances:

  • Payment processors — when submitting recovery requests or dispute evidence on your behalf, we share only the specific transaction data necessary to support that action.
  • Service providers — we use trusted infrastructure providers (including cloud storage, database, and communication tools) that process data strictly on our behalf and under confidentiality obligations.
  • Legal requirements — we may disclose information if required by law, court order, or regulatory authority.
  • Business transfers — in the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your data may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify you in advance.

06

Data Retention

We retain your data for as long as necessary to deliver our services and meet our legal obligations. Specifically:

  • Transaction and anomaly data is retained for 24 months from the date of collection to support ongoing monitoring and dispute windows.
  • Recovery action records and audit trails are retained for 5 years to support regulatory and financial audit requirements.
  • Upon termination of our engagement, you may request deletion of your data. We will complete deletion within 30 days, excluding records we are required to retain by law.

07

Security

We implement technical and organizational measures designed to protect your data against unauthorized access, loss, or disclosure. These measures include:

  • Encryption of data at rest and in transit (TLS 1.2+)
  • Encrypted storage of all API credentials
  • Role-based access controls limiting internal access to data
  • Audit logging of all data access and recovery actions
  • Regular security reviews of our infrastructure and processes

No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure. If you believe your information has been compromised, please contact us immediately at Team@coreregulation.com.

08

Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights regarding your data:

  • Access — request a copy of the data we hold about you or your organization.
  • Correction — request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Deletion — request deletion of your data upon termination of our engagement, subject to legal retention requirements.
  • Portability — request your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Objection — object to certain uses of your data.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at Team@coreregulation.com. We will respond within 30 days.

09

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "last updated" date at the top of this page and notify active clients via email. Continued use of our services after changes are posted constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.
